<p><p>Cell and gene therapy manufacturing quality control (QC) encompasses several critical testing types to ensure product safety and efficacy. Safety testing evaluates the absence of harmful contaminants, while purity testing assesses the separation of the therapeutic agent from impurities. Potency testing confirms the therapeutic effect remains intact, identity testing verifies the correct formulation, and stability testing examines the product's shelf life and response to storage conditions. Together, these tests ensure that therapies meet regulatory standards and patient safety requirements.</p></p>
