<p><p>Superalloys are advanced materials designed for high-temperature applications, particularly in the nuclear market. They are categorized into three primary types: Fe-based, Ni-based, and Co-based superalloys. Fe-based superalloys offer good strength and corrosion resistance, making them suitable for less extreme conditions. Ni-based superalloys excel at high temperatures and exhibit superior creep resistance, ideal for nuclear reactors. Co-based superalloys provide exceptional strength and oxidation resistance, often used in environments involving extreme thermal and mechanical stresses. Together, they serve critical roles in nuclear technology.</p></p>
